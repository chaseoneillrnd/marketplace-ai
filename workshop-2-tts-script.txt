Before you teach Claude what matters, tell it what to ignore.

There is a file called dot claudeignore. Same syntax as dot gitignore. And here is why it matters.

Your package lock file. Just that one file. Thirty thousand to eighty thousand tokens. By itself. A single file eating somewhere between fifteen and forty percent of Claude's entire context window before Claude has read a single line of your actual code.

Your node modules directory. Your build output. Your test snapshots. Your environment files. Your lock files. Your media assets. Every single one of them in the window unless you say otherwise.

There are three categories to filter. Secrets. Your env files, your key files, your P E M files. Claude should never see these, ever. Dependencies. Node modules, Python cache, vendor directories. Concept four.

Every technical decision you make today, you will forget the reasoning for in six months. Not might forget. Will forget. Your teammate will forget faster. Your new hire will have no idea.

An A D R. Architecture Decision Record. Four paragraphs. Context. Decision. Consequences. Alternatives considered.

Context is one sentence. What problem forced a decision. Decision is one sentence. What we chose. Consequences is two lists. What this enables and what this costs. Alternatives considered is a table. Option we looked at. Why we rejected it.

That last part. Alternatives considered. That is the part people skip. That is also the part that saves your future self from relitigating the same decision next quarter.

Here is the move. When the decision is fresh, write it down. Four paragraphs. Commit it to docs slash agentic slash DECISIONS dot M D. Claude reads the file. Claude now knows why the code is the way it is.

One more thing about A D Rs. This is not an engineer-only concept. If you are a product manager, write A D Rs for your product decisions. If you are an analyst, write them for your methodology choices. If you make decisions that compound, write them down in this format. Claude will read them. So will the next human who inherits your work.
